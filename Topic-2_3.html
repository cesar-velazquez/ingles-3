Child prodigies

Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz
Juana Ramírez de Asbaje, better known as Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz was born in San Miguel Nepantla in the Viceroyalty of New Spain (now Mexico).
She showed remarkable intellectual potential early on.
She could read at the age of three.
She could write dramatic poems before when she was eight.
But she couldn’t receive formal education due to her family’s limited finances.
However, she became one of the most famous writers in the history of Mexico.

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was the child prodigy par excellence.
He could play songs on the harpsichord at the age of 4.
He could also compose simple music at five.
When he was seven years old, the Mozart family went on the first of several tours to demonstrate the prodigious musical abilities of the young marvel and his sister Maria Anna.

Shirley Temple
Shirley Temple was a famous singing and acting prodigy.
Born in 1928, in California, she began her film career at the tender age of three.
She could act and dance tap when she was five.
She received a special Academy Award at the age of seven.
She continued her career as the queen of the show for several years.
Ultimately though, she quit movies and joined politics, and died in 2014.

Could

We use could when we talk about past abilities.

Examples:

  • Could for ability.
  • My sister could hold a spider in his hands when she was a child. Now, she hates them.
  • I couldn’t travel to Egypt last summer because my father didn’t pay for the trip.
  • Linda could fix a car when she was a young. Nowadays, she always goes to the car service.
  • Could your cousin climb the Everest when he lived in Nepal?
  • No, he couldn’t.

Could is a modal verb. Like all modals, could come before the base form of the verbs. They also have the same form with all subjects.

The 20th century marked a new era of freedom for women. This century saw the ratification of the 19th amendment, which gave women the right to vote in the USA. It also started a new era of social liberation that brought females some other basic rights. And while they are still fighting for equality, these were some norms for women at the beginning of this century:

Most women married at a very young age and they could not decide the number of children they wanted to have because there were no contraceptive methods. It was until 1972 that birth control became accessible to all women.

Working women couldn’t get pregnant because they could get fired and they couldn’t fight against sexual harassment because courts didn't recognize it was a crime, until 1980.

Most single women at that time couldn't get a credit card. Only a married woman could get one if her husband was willing to sign it. It was until 1974 that it became illegal to deny a woman a credit card on the basis of gender.

Most women couldn't also access prestigious education. It wasn’t an option even for the brightest women of that time. They couldn’t also receive military training. The first half of the 20th century saw two major wars, but both of these epic conflicts had no women in combat. They could only serve in traditionally female roles, such as nursing.

And of course, women couldn’t vote in any kind of elections until 1920; when the passing of the 19th Amendment in the USA started a big movement that was echoed in other nations such as Mexico, where women could vote for the first time in 1955.
