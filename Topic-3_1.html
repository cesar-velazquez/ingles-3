Topic 3.1 What were you doing?

What were you doing?

Past Continuous

Look at the following pictures. What were they doing last weekend?

I was studying for my English exam at 11 o’clock last Saturday.

Peter and his friends were playing soccer this time last Sunday.

My dad was making a delicious dinner after work.

My sisters were watching a horror movie at midnight on Sunday.

As you can see the previous examples are formed by the past tense of the verb

to be + action with the ing form.

This is called Past Continuous, and we use it to express:

1. Actions that were in progress at a special time in the past.

  • I was studying for my English exam at 11 o’clock last Saturday.

    to be + action with the ing form.

    was to be studying action with the ing form.

  • Peter and his friends were playing soccer this time last Sunday.

    to be + action with the ing form.

    were to be playing action with the ing form.

2. Repeated actions over a past period of time.

  • Last summer Tony was practicing the guitar three times a day.

    to be + action with the ing form.

    was to be practicing action with the ing form.

  • My friends were chatting every day.

    to be + action with the ing form.

    were to be chatting action with the ing form.

3. Something changes or grows.

  • I was in hurry to get school because it was getting late. (The time was changing).

    to be + action with the ing form.

    was to be getting action with the ing form.

  • My plants were growing quickly last spring.

    to be + action with the ing form.

    were to be growing action with the ing form.

Note: we often use one auxiliary with more than one ing form if the subject of the verbs remains the same.

  • The boy and the girl were sitting in the car, just staring at each other

    to be + action with the ing form.

    were to be sitting action with the ing form.

It is very important to pay attention to the following charts where you will find the grammar structure in its three different forms: affirmative, negative and interrogative.

In most of the verbs you add -ing.

  • Cooking
  • Playing
  • Sleeping

But in some cases you have to change the verb.

Verbs ending in: consonant + vowel + consonant, double the final letter.

  • run > running
  • plan > planning

Verbs ending in: “e” you delete “e” and add ing

  • come > coming
  • make > making

Examples:

  • Were you working yesterday afternoon?

    Yes, I was.

  • Was Mr. Garcia studying all night?

    No, he wasn’t.

You can also make questions to get more information. Just add wh- at the beginning of the interrogative sentence.

Examples:

  • Where were you working yesterday afternoon?

    I was working at the hospital.

  • Who was driving your car last night?

    My brother was.

Note: There are certain verbs that you can’t use with the past continuous.

These are the stative verbs.

Stative verbs

  • Dislike
  • Hate
  • Like
  • Love
  • Need
  • Prefer
  • Want
