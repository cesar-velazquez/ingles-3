There was - there were

We use there was or there were to indicate someone or something existed in a certain place.
In a statement, a noun always follows there was or there were.
There was and there were represent the past tense form of “there is” and “there are”.

I didn’t eat my soup because there was a fly in it. Yuck!

Use there was with a singular count noun or a noncount noun.

There were a lot of people at the concert.

Use there were with a plural noun.

There was and there were represent the past tense form of “there is” and “there are”.
